    <h2>Understanding the Technology</h2>
    
    <details class="collapsible-section">
      <summary>What Makes AI "Agentic"?</summary>
      <div class="section-content">
        <p>Traditional AI systems are reactive—they respond to prompts and produce outputs. Agentic AI is fundamentally different:</p>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Goal-Oriented:</strong> Agents work toward objectives, not just responses</li>
          <li><strong>Autonomous Planning:</strong> They break complex tasks into steps</li>
          <li><strong>Tool Usage:</strong> Agents can use APIs, databases, and external services</li>
          <li><strong>Persistent Memory:</strong> They remember context across interactions</li>
          <li><strong>Self-Correction:</strong> Agents can evaluate and improve their approach</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </details>

    <details class="collapsible-section">
      <summary>Key Technologies Enabling Agentic AI</summary>
      <div class="section-content">
        <div class="tech-grid">
          <div class="tech-item">
            <h4>Large Language Models (LLMs)</h4>
            <p>Foundation models like GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini provide reasoning capabilities</p>
          </div>
          <div class="tech-item">
            <h4>Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)</h4>
            <p>Enables agents to access and use external knowledge sources</p>
          </div>
          <div class="tech-item">
            <h4>Function Calling & Tool Use</h4>
            <p>Allows agents to interact with external APIs and services</p>
          </div>
          <div class="tech-item">
            <h4>Vector Databases</h4>
            <p>Provides semantic search and long-term memory capabilities</p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </details>
